Project Info

This is a group project with friends at the university. This game is a local 2 vs. 2 mech battle where each team must co-operate a single mech to take down the other team. Each team consists of a pilot and an engineer, each with their own respective UI. At the moment, the game is designed to be played with exactly 4 players and requires all players to be on the same local network. Players communicate via shouting at each other in real life.

The pilot gets to see the mech from a top down third-person POV and is in charge of shooting, moving and dodging enemy attacks. There are also energy cells randomly scattered throughout the map when the game starts. When the pilot collects these energy cells, the engineer can use them to convert into health, fuel or ammo. The mech movement is controlled via and analog stick that appears when the player touches anywhere on the left side of the screen as a pilot. The mech shoots alternating shots from its arms when the right side of the screen is touched by the player. The pilot does not have the ability to see the mech's health, fuel, or ammo. (This is intentional)

The engineer can only see the mech from the inside, therefore, the UI consists of the mech's stats such as health, fuel, ammo, etc. The engineer's job is to tell the pilot the status of the mech so that the pilot can choose whether or not to engage an enemy. When an engineer wants to convert the energy cells, they must drag the energy cells into one of the converters in which a bar will start to rise. Engineers can convert up to 3 energy cells simultaneously. In the mini-game, the engineer has to time the bars correctly to get the maximum benefit from the energy cells. When the mech runs out of fuel, the mech will break down and it is up to the engineer to restart the mech once again via a number sequence mini-game.
